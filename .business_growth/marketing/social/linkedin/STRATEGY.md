## Posting Cadence

**Target: 3-4 original posts/week**

| Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Long-form text posts (4-8 paragraphs) | 2x/week | Core thought leadership, follower growth |
| Shorter takes (2-3 paragraphs) | 1-2x/week | Engagement, discoverability |
| Carousel/document posts | 1-2x/month | High engagement format, save-worthy |
| Reposts with commentary | 1-2x/week | Borrow audience, show engagement |
| Quality comments on others' posts | 5-10/day | **Primary growth lever** |

### Timing
- Post between 8-10 AM EST (US morning, Israel afternoon) - Tue through Thu
- Secondary window: 12-2 PM EST on Mon/Fri
- Tuesday-Thursday posts get highest LinkedIn engagement
- Avoid weekends for original content

### Post Structure
Every LinkedIn post should follow this structure:
1. **Hook line** - Provocative or surprising statement (this shows before "see more")
2. **Body** - 4-8 short paragraphs, heavy white space between lines
3. **Closer** - Final insight or question (NOT "thoughts?" or "agree?")
4. **Hashtags** - Max 3, placed at bottom, not inline

## Comment Game (Primary Growth Lever)

Even with 2,976 followers, commenting on larger accounts accelerates growth significantly on LinkedIn.

### Process
1. Follow 15-20 accounts in your space (see engagement-targets.md)
2. Turn on post notifications for top 10
3. Comment within 1 hour of their posts with genuine insight
4. 5-10 quality comments per day
5. Your comments appear in your network's feed, expanding reach

### Comment Quality Guidelines
- Add a genuine insight, contrarian angle, or concrete example
- Comments should read like standalone mini-posts (3-5 sentences minimum)
- Match the tone of the account you're commenting on
- Early comments get algorithmic boost - speed matters
- Never use "Great post!" or "Love this!" - add substance
- Share a personal experience or observation that extends the author's point

### LinkedIn-Specific Comment Tactics
- Tag relevant people when it adds value (not spam tagging)
- Longer comments (50+ words) perform better on LinkedIn
- Comments with line breaks are more readable and get more likes
- Asking a genuine follow-up question extends the thread
