
# Goal

Get 10 design partners in 1 niche who will:
	1.	connect repo + inbox,
	2.	use it on real tickets for 2 to 4 weeks,
	3.	give weekly feedback and a testimonial if it works.

Design partners are not “free users.” They are early customers who co-build with you.

⸻

1) Pick the easiest niche for design partners

Start with a niche where “code grounded support” is obviously valuable:

Best first niche: B2B SaaS with integrations or APIs
Signals:
	•	webhooks, SDKs, API docs
	•	“integration support” pages
	•	public changelog with frequent fixes
	•	founders answering tickets in public (Intercom widget, shared inbox mentions, community posts)

Avoid for first batch:
	•	mostly billing/account support
	•	consumer products
	•	anything with heavy compliance review

⸻

2) Define “ideal design partner” criteria

You want teams that are small enough to move fast, but large enough to feel pain.

Must have
	•	2 to 15 people
	•	GitHub
	•	Intercom or Zendesk or a shared email inbox
	•	at least 10 support conversations per week (even 5 can work if technical)

Strong signals
	•	recurring technical questions
	•	founder or engineer frequently pulled into support
	•	fast release cadence

Hard no
	•	expects perfect autonomy from day 1
	•	cannot connect repo for security reasons
	•	wants enterprise procurement up front

⸻

3) Build a target list of 80 to 150 prospects

You only need 10. Assume 5 to 15% conversion if you do it well.

Where to find them
	•	GitHub Marketplace style products and OSS maintainers who also sell SaaS
	•	Intercom and Zendesk app directories: find companies using those tools, then identify founders
	•	Product Hunt launches that mention “API”, “developer tool”, “integration”
	•	Indie Hackers “product” pages in B2B SaaS categories
	•	“Powered by Intercom” sites for technical products
	•	Founders on X posting about support load, bugs, shipping, context switching

What to capture in a sheet
	•	company, niche
	•	founder name + contact
	•	support channel they use
	•	why they are a fit (one line)
	•	last activity signal (recent launch, hiring support, recent bugfix posts)

⸻

4) Your offer needs to be concrete

Design partners say yes when the terms feel fair and specific.

Offer (recommended)
	•	30 to 45 day design partner program
	•	free during program
	•	then $50/mo locked for 12 months if they stay on (or 50% off Pro)
	•	weekly 20 minute feedback call (or async voice notes)
	•	you will personally set it up and tune it to their product

What you ask for
	•	connect repo + inbox
	•	let you review 10 to 20 tickets to tune prompts and workflows
	•	permission to publish a short case study if successful

⸻

5) Outreach that converts (two sequences)

A) Founder DM sequence (X, LinkedIn, Slack)

Message 1
“Hey {Name} I’m building support that answers with code context and turns real tickets into a clean GitHub issue or even a PR. Looks like you ship fast and handle technical questions. Want to try it as a design partner for 30 days? I’ll set it up personally. If it works, you keep $50/mo locked for a year.”

If they reply “tell me more”
“Setup is GitHub + your inbox. It learns from the repo, so answers reference real implementation. When a ticket matters, it drafts a spec and issue with repro steps and context. Goal is fewer engineer interrupts. Can I send a 3 minute demo or hop on a 15 minute call?”

Nudge (48 hours later)
“Quick bump. I’m picking 10 teams this month and prioritizing API and integrations products. If support steals engineering time, this will be immediately useful.”

B) Email sequence (works well when you can personalize)

Subject options:
	•	“Stop forwarding support to engineering”
	•	“Ticket → GitHub issue in 1 click”
	•	“Support that reads your repo”

Email 1:
“Hey {Name},
Noticed {specific signal: API, integrations, webhooks, SDK}. I’m building a support system that reads the repo and answers technical tickets with implementation context. When something is worth fixing, it turns the conversation into a clean spec and a GitHub issue with repro steps, and optionally a reviewable PR.

I’m recruiting 10 design partners. I’ll set it up with GitHub + {Intercom/Zendesk/email} and tune it to your product. Free for 30 days, then $50/mo locked for a year if you keep it.

Worth a quick 15 minutes this week?”

Follow-up 1 (3 days):
“Wanted to sanity check fit. Do you ever pull an engineer into support to answer ‘how does this work’ or debug issues? If yes, you’re the exact team this is for.”

Follow-up 2 (7 days):
“If now isn’t right, no worries. If you know a 2 to 10 person B2B SaaS where support constantly interrupts shipping, I’d love an intro.”

⸻

6) The “design partner call” agenda (15 to 20 minutes)

Keep it tight. You’re qualifying, not pitching.
	1.	What support channel, how many conversations per week
	2.	Top 5 technical ticket types
	3.	What happens today when support is stuck
	4.	Define success: “reduce escalations by X”, “cut time to issue by Y”
	5.	Security comfort: repo access scope, which repos, any exclusions
	6.	Next step: book setup session and pick a starting inbox

Close with a crisp commitment
“Let’s run this for 30 days. You’ll use it on real tickets, we do weekly feedback, and we measure whether it reduces engineering interrupts.”

⸻

7) Onboarding flow for design partners

You win or lose here.

Setup session (30 to 45 minutes)
	•	connect GitHub repo
	•	connect inbox
	•	choose “safe mode” rules:
	•	always cite code references
	•	if uncertain, ask 1 clarifying question or escalate
	•	never invent endpoints or behavior
	•	tag 10 recent tickets and run through them live
	•	configure escalation output: issue template, labels, assignee, priority

AHA moment you must produce in week 1
	•	one answer grounded in code that solves a real ticket
	•	one “issue draft” created from a real conversation with repro steps and file pointers

⸻

8) Feedback loop that keeps them engaged

Weekly, ask only these:
	1.	What was the single best ticket it handled?
	2.	What was the worst failure?
	3.	What would make you pay $50/mo without thinking?

Also send a weekly digest:
	•	tickets answered
	•	escalations drafted
	•	top recurring issues detected
	•	suggested fixes

⸻

9) Targets and math (so you don’t drift)

To get 10 design partners:
	•	Build list: 120 prospects
	•	Outreach: 20 per day for 6 days
	•	Expect: 20 to 30 replies
	•	Calls: 12 to 18
	•	Design partners: 8 to 12

If conversion is lower, the usual fix is more personalization and a narrower niche.

	•	1 sentence: “Support that reads your repo and turns tickets into shipped fixes”
	•	who it’s for: 2 to 15 person B2B SaaS with technical support
	•	what they get: setup + tuning + locked pricing
	•	what you need: repo + inbox + weekly feedback
	•	apply form: repo stack, inbox, ticket volume, biggest pain
